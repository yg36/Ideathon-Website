    • THINK INNOVATE IMPACT

    An Ideathon represents a structured event wherein participants engage in collaborative ideation processes to conceive and refine innovative concepts within a defined timeframe. These sessions typically revolve around problem-solving exercises or thematic explorations, fostering brainstorming, group discussions, and iterative idea development. Participants subsequently present their refined ideas to a panel of judges or mentors for evaluation and feedback. Ideathons serve as platforms for fostering creativity and entrepreneurial thinking across diverse domains, including technology, business, social innovation, and entrepreneurship.

    Themes:
    Empowering Ideas,Shaping Tommorow

    1.Health and Well-being

    This topic focuses on promoting physical, mental, and emotional wellness. Ideas could include apps for tracking health metrics, telemedicine platforms, mental health support tools, and initiatives for promoting healthy lifestyles.

    2.Agritech for Sustainability

    Agritech involves using technology to improve agricultural productivity and sustainability. Ideas might revolve around precision farming techniques, IoT sensors for monitoring crops and soil health, sustainable farming practices, and platforms for connecting farmers with markets.

    3.Eco-friendly Innovations

    This topic concerns innovations that reduce environmental impact. Ideas could include renewable energy solutions, biodegradable packaging, sustainable transportation options, waste management systems, and eco-friendly manufacturing processes.

    4. Education and E-learning:

    This topic revolves around leveraging technology to improve education access and quality. Ideas could include online learning platforms, interactive educational apps, virtual classrooms, teacher training programs, and initiatives for bridging the digital divide in education.

    5.Digital Governance

    This topic revolves around leveraging technology to improve education access and quality. Ideas could include online learning platforms, interactive educational apps, virtual classrooms, teacher training programs, and initiatives for bridging the digital divide in education.

    6. Inclusive Technology

    Inclusive technology focuses on designing products and services that are accessible to all, regardless of ability or background. Ideas might include assistive technologies for people with disabilities, inclusive design principles in digital products, and initiatives to promote diversity in the tech industry.

    7.Financial Inclusion

    This topic aims to provide access to financial services for underserved populations. Ideas could include mobile banking solutions, microfinance initiatives, digital payment platforms, credit scoring algorithms for the unbanked, and financial literacy programs.

    8. Build for Community

    This involves creating solutions that strengthen local communities and foster social connections. Ideas might include community-driven crowdfunding platforms, neighborhood sharing economies, civic engagement apps, and platforms for connecting volunteers with opportunities.

    9. Smart Infrastructure

    Smart infrastructure utilizes technology to improve the efficiency and sustainability of urban infrastructure. Ideas could include smart grid systems, intelligent transportation networks, water management solutions, IoT-based infrastructure monitoring, and resilient urban planning.

    10.Open Innovation

    Open innovation emphasizes collaboration and sharing of ideas across organizations and sectors. Ideas might include open-source software projects, collaborative research platforms, crowdsourcing initiatives for problem-solving, and hackathons for tackling social challenges.

    11.Contribution to PUP

    It refers to brainstorming and implementing ideas to improve and enhance Punjabi University Patiala (PUP) campus. This involves identifying areas within the university ecosystem that require attention.The goal is to propel PUP towards greater excellence and contribute positively to the growth and development of the university community.

    Rules & Regulations

    • A participating team can have a maximum of 2 members
    • Only shortlisted candidates will be allowed to present on the event day
    • Any idea presented should align with atleast one of the defined themes
    • Shortlisted candidates must bring their own laptops on the event day
    • Ideas should be presented in the form of PPTs
    • Registeration fees will be non-refunable under any case
    • The ideas or the content presented should be unique
    • Only the students of Punjabi University Patiala are allowed to participate in the contest
    • Students from all the departments of University are allowed to participate
    • In case of any conflict, decision made by Jury will be considered final
    • Any registered startup is not eligible for the event
    • A participant can be the member of any one team only
    • No switching of teams will be allowed after registration
